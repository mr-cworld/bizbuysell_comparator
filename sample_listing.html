Business Description

Engineered Sourcing. Trusted Execution. Long-Term Clients.

Specialty Contract Manufacturer with Longstanding Clients, High Margins, and Scalable Global Supply Chain

ManuCo is a U.S.-led contract manufacturing and sourcing firm specializing in the delivery of custom-engineered, tight-tolerance components for medical, cleanroom, and industrial clients. With more than two decades of experience, the company operates as an extension of its customers’ engineering and procurement functions—offering high-quality, compliance-ready products sourced through a vetted Asia-based supplier network.

What Sets It Apart?
ManuCo is not a trading company or a general importer. It is a purpose-built sourcing and fulfillment platform with embedded QA/QC processes, direct factory relationships (no intermediaries), and demonstrated execution in highly regulated industries. Its Asia-based execution team manages day-to-day production while the U.S. team maintains client relationships, oversees compliance documentation, and ensures delivery performance.

2024 Performance
• Revenue: $5.94M
• Gross Profit: $1.91M (32.1% margin)
• Adjusted EBITDA: $247K (after market-based compensation adjustment for the owner)
• SDE: $482K
• 2025 YTD Revenue (Q1): $2.75M – trending toward record annual performance

Client Profile & Retention
The company serves OEMs and distributors who rely on consistent, high-quality components that meet tight specifications. Many clients have remained with the business for 10–20 years, driven by the value of proprietary tooling, engineering support, and high switching costs associated with re-sourcing compliant suppliers. The company’s five largest clients accounted for ~90% of revenue in 2024—each representing long-term relationships with consistent reorder patterns.

Operating Model
ManuCo operates with a lean team structure. Core functions include bilingual project managers based in Asia, U.S.-based leadership, and fulfillment oversight via a leased warehouse in Columbus, OH. The business uses structured SOPs, centralized quoting, and custom specification management to ensure consistency across projects and geographies. Fulfillment is compatible with 3PL handoff or in-house logistics models depending on buyer preference.

Assets & Infrastructure
• No owned real estate; asset-light, leased warehouse arrangement
• Custom tooling and BOM documentation for key clients
• QA/QC checklists and supplier audit protocols
• Relationships with over 50 factories across China, Vietnam, Taiwan, and India
• Established client relationships and pricing histories
• Digital systems for quoting, coordination, and fulfillment

Why Buyers Will Be Interested
• Premium gross margins driven by specialty products—not commoditized items
• Embedded customer relationships with technical and logistical integration
• Scalable sourcing engine and fulfillment platform
• No capex needed to grow: infrastructure and team already in place
• Opportunity to add sales infrastructure and increase margin through SG&A leverage
• Flexible transition support from the owner, who is exiting for personal reasons after many years of successful stewardship

Ideal Buyer
ManuCo is well-suited for strategic buyers in manufacturing, sourcing, or OEM verticals who want to bolt on a specialty supply chain capability. It also fits well with PE-backed platforms or family offices seeking stable, cash-flowing, asset-light businesses with upside potential.

Transition Plan
The seller is committed to a smooth transition and is offering 3–6 months of post-sale support, including client introductions, operational handoff, and training on supplier and QA systems.

Facilities:
ManuCo operates as an asset-light business with minimal owned equipment and no fixed manufacturing footprint. The company leases warehouse space which supports: • Drop-ship fulfillment for U.S.-based customers • Inbound logistics coordination from Asia-based suppliers • Basic inventory handling and staging (no long-term warehousing or heavy assembly) There is no owned real estate and no physical plant—this operational structure is designed to maximize flexibility and minimize fixed costs. Key Assets Include: • Proprietary SOPs for sourcing, QA/QC
Competition:
ManuCo operates in the fragmented contract manufacturing and sourcing space, serving U.S.-based clients who require custom, non-commodity components. Its competitive edge lies in tight-tolerance, compliance-driven sourcing, high customer retention, and above-average gross margins. Unlike trading firms, ManuCo acts as a technical partner, not a broker, and competes on execution quality, supplier transparency, and integration with client engineering and procurement teams.
Growth & Expansion:
ManuCo is well-positioned for growth through expanded sales outreach, deeper penetration into existing accounts, and targeted vertical expansion in medical, cleanroom, and mobility sectors. The sourcing platform is scalable, with room to add U.S.-based assembly or warehousing. A strategic buyer with sales infrastructure or adjacent customer relationships can unlock top-line growth while leveraging the company’s proven supply chain and project management systems.
